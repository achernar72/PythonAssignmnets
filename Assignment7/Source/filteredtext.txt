 "Despacito" (American Spanish: [despa'sito]; English: "Slowly") single Puerto Rican singer Luis Fonsi featuring Puerto Rican rapper Daddy Yankee former's upcoming studio album.[1] On January 12, 2017, Universal Music Latin released "Despacito" music video, shows artists performing song La Perla neighborhood Old San Juan, Puerto Rico local bar La Factoría. The song written Luis Fonsi, Erika Ender Daddy Yankee, produced Andrés Torres Mauricio Rengifo.[2] It reggaeton-pop song composed common time lyrics sexual relationship, performed smooth romantic way. Commercially, song topped charts 45 countries reached top 10 9 others, making Fonsi Daddy Yankee's successful single date. It became first song primarily Spanish top Billboard Hot 100 since "Macarena" (Bayside Boys Mix) 1996. The official video "Despacito" YouTube received one billionth view April 20, 2017 97 days, becoming second-fastest video site reach milestone behind Adele's "Hello".[3] It received two billionth view June 16 154 days, making fastest video site reach milestone. With 3.3 million certified sales plus track-equivalent streams, "Despacito" one best-selling Latin singles United States. Several remix versions released following single's success. On March 17, 2017, solo pop version salsa version featuring Puerto Rican musician Victor Manuelle released. On April 17, 2017, remix version featuring Canadian singer Justin Bieber released; version helped improve song's chart performance numerous countries, particularly English-speaking world. On May 5, 2017, electronic version produced American trio Major Lazer Colombian DJ MOSKA urban version produced Sky released. In July, ”Despacito” became world’s streamed song time 4.6 billion streams.
